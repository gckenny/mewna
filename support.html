Frequently Asked Questions

How does Mewna work?

Mewna uses Cole-Kripke (1992) actigraphy on iPhone motion data, plus your Apple Watch's heart rate, HRV, and sleep stages from HealthKit, to generate a sleep score and human-readable analysis each morning. Everything happens on your device.

Do I need an Apple Watch?

No. The phone-only mode (place iPhone by your pillow) works on its own. Apple Watch makes the analysis more accurate by adding heart rate, HRV, and watchOS sleep stages.

How accurate is it?

Cole-Kripke shows ~89% agreement with PSG (polysomnography) in adults. With Apple Watch, accuracy further improves. That said, Mewna is not a medical device — for clinical concerns, consult a sleep doctor.

Will it drain my battery?

Mewna keeps the screen alive only during a tracking session. We recommend plugging in. iPhone-only mode uses ~10–15% overnight on iPhone 15 Pro on charger.

What if I get up at night?

On the tracking screen, tap "Pause." Doing your thing, then tap "Resume" when you're back in bed. Pauses don't count toward sleep efficiency.

Where is my data?

On your iPhone (SwiftData) and, if you're signed into iCloud, in your private CloudKit container. Mewna has no servers — your data never reaches us. See Privacy Policy.

How do I export or delete my data?

Export: Settings → Export data (JSON). Delete: Settings → Erase all records. Both are reversible only by re-importing your JSON, so handle with care.

Can I use Mewna on Apple Watch standalone?

Yes. The Mewna Watch app starts a workout session and writes sleep + heart-rate data directly to HealthKit. The iPhone reads it on next sync.

What languages are supported?

Traditional Chinese · Simplified Chinese · English · Japanese. Switch in Settings → Language at any time.
